Product Discovery
3 Universal Discovery Frameworks
Framework 1  —  PSID
Use when: prompt is open-ended  ("how would you discover what to build for X customer?")
P
Problem Space
Name the broad customer pain. Resist jumping to solutions. "Customers calling in about balance confusion" not "we need a dashboard."
S
Segment
Narrow to one persona before going deep. "I'd focus on the revolver who carries $2K+ and calls in monthly — not the transactor."
I
Insight Method
How would you learn the real cause? Call transcript analysis, support ticket clustering, session replay, 1:1 interviews with reps and customers.
D
Direction Hypothesis
Based on what you'd expect to find, state your hypothesis for where to play — and what would change it.
Capital One signal: Name the data sources you'd actually use at C1 — IVR logs, servicing call transcripts, digital drop-off funnels, NPS verbatims.
Framework 2  —  Jobs-to-be-Done + Friction Map
Use when: given a specific product area  ("design a feature for cardholders who carry a balance")
1
State the Job
"When I [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."   Ex: "When I see a charge I don't recognize, I want instant clarity, so I can decide whether to dispute without calling."
2
Map the Friction
Where does the current experience fail that job? Walk through: Awareness → Action → Resolution → Trust. Pick the highest-friction moment.
3
Design Toward It
Propose a solution targeted at that single friction point. Breadth of ideas shows creativity; deliberate narrowing shows maturity.
4
Validation Criteria
What signal tells you the job is being done? (Call deflection rate, digital resolution %, time-to-resolution, CSAT on that flow)
Capital One signal: Tie validation metrics to servicing KPIs C1 actually cares about — call deflection, digital containment, first-contact resolution, NPS on servicing interactions.
Framework 3  —  NOW / NEXT / NEVER
Use when: you need to show prioritization discipline after generating ideas
✓
NOW
Solves the validated pain, low regulatory risk, fast learning loop. Can ship or test in the near term.
→
NEXT
High potential but needs more discovery, has dependencies, or regulatory review required before proceeding.
✕
NEVER (for now)
Solves a different problem than what we validated. Intellectually interesting but not addressing the job-to-be-done.
Capital One signal: Saying "never for now" out loud signals you're not in love with your own ideas — a senior PM behavior. In a regulated bank, also flag compliance review as a gating factor for NEXT-tier ideas.

Case Math Quick Reference — 6 Core Formulas
Breakeven Units
Fixed Cost ÷ (Price − Var Cost)
$500K ÷ ($50 − $20) = 16,667 units
Expected Value
Σ (Probability × Outcome)
0.6×$200K + 0.4×−$80K = $88K
Weighted Average
Σ (Weight × Value) ÷ Σ Weights
0.7×92 + 0.3×78 = 87.8
Market Sizing
Population × TAM% × Price
330M × 4% × $120 = $1.6B
Fixed vs Variable Cost
Total = Fixed + (VarCost × Units)
$200K + ($15 × 50K) = $950K
★ Avg Lifetime / LTV
Lifetime = 1 ÷ Churn Rate
1÷0.05 = 20 mo  ·  $8×20 = $160 LTV
Portfolio: 200K × $160 = $32M

Example 1  ·  AI / ML Product Leadership
Built ML-Powered Search Platform from Zero — NPS +42, 22% Search Completion Lift
Situation
Amazon's internal catalog had fragmented discovery across multiple systems. Users couldn't find what they needed, driving downstream procurement errors and friction.
Task
Own zero-to-one build of a unified ML search platform, leading a team of 5 engineers (Data Science + Backend) with no existing infrastructure to build on.
Action
Designed NLP-based entity extraction and attribute weighting across 30K+ SKUs. Built an experimentation roadmap to tune relevance scoring iteratively. Established metadata quality standards with Finance and Legal for auditability.
Result
NPS of +42. Relevance scoring improved 28%. CTR up 18%. Successful search completion up 22%.
C1 →
Capital One's servicing channels face the same problem: customers who can't find answers self-serve in app or IVR call in. This story demonstrates I know how to build intelligent search that reduces friction at scale — directly applicable to dispute lookup, payment assistance, and reward redemption flows.
Example 2  ·  Platform Strategy at Scale
Owned 3-Year Roadmap for Enterprise Platform Serving 750,000+ Users — 27% YoY Defect Reduction
Situation
An internal procurement platform serving 750K+ Amazon corporate employees had accrued significant technical debt and compliance risk, with no unified product strategy.
Task
Set and execute a 3-year roadmap, ship 20+ features focused on spend governance and compliance, and drive measurable quality improvements at global scale.
Action
Prioritized spend governance and auditability as platform foundation. Launched QuickSight/SQL dashboards to instrument system health. Led global rollout of purchase request system, onboarding 10,000+ users with PO validation.
Result
27% YoY reduction in defects. 10,000+ users onboarded globally. Audit-ready traceability established across Finance, Legal, and Engineering stakeholders.
C1 →
Capital One's card servicing platform serves millions of cardholders under regulatory scrutiny. This story shows I've owned long-horizon product strategy, driven quality at massive scale, and built compliance into the product — not bolted it on after.
Example 3  ·  Operational Transformation in a Regulated Environment
Reduced Government Procurement Cycle from 28 Days to 5 Days Through Process Redesign
Situation
AWS's government procurement process averaged 28 days per cycle — creating delays in data center operations and risk exposure in a compliance-critical environment.
Task
Redesign the procurement workflow to eliminate cycle time while maintaining full regulatory compliance and auditability for government contracts.
Action
Implemented standardized Master Work Orders as a reusable compliance template. Trained internal teams on compliant workflows to eliminate rework loops. Partnered with Legal to ensure every shortcut was a safe shortcut.
Result
Cycle time dropped from 28 days to 5 days — an 82% reduction — without any compliance exceptions. Process adopted across the organization.
C1 →
Customer servicing is fundamentally about cycle time under compliance pressure — dispute resolution, hardship processing, account corrections. This story shows I redesign complex, regulated workflows for speed without creating risk, which is the exact tension a CS PM at Capital One manages daily.
Example 4  ·  Strategic Build vs. Buy Decision
Led Buy-vs-Build Analysis for AI-Native Sourcing Platform — Evaluated 3 Enterprise Vendors on TCO, Fit, and Risk
Situation
Amazon needed to modernize procurement workflows for 2,000+ global users. Leadership was split between building in-house and acquiring an AI-native SaaS platform.
Task
Lead a rigorous buy-vs-build evaluation, synthesize findings for executive decision-making, and align Engineering, Finance, and Legal on the path forward.
Action
Ran structured analysis across Fairmarkit, Zip, and TealBook — evaluating technical fit, integration complexity, and TCO. Facilitated cross-functional sessions to surface compliance requirements and data security constraints that eliminated two candidates.
Result
Delivered a clear recommendation with executive alignment. Cross-functional team — Engineering, Finance, Legal — committed to a single direction without escalation.
C1 →
Senior PM roles at Capital One regularly face build-vs-buy decisions for servicing technology — IVR systems, CRM tools, AI-assist platforms for agents. This story demonstrates I drive those decisions with rigor, bring the right stakeholders along, and reach alignment without paralysis.
